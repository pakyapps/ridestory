This Privacy Policy explains how RideStory (“RideStory”, “we”, “us” or “our”) handles personal data when you use the RideStory mobile application, website and related support services (together, the “Services”).

RideStory is the controller of personal data described in this policy. RideStory does not require or create user accounts. Questions and privacy requests can be submitted through the support form.

1. Data we process

The data we process depends on the features you use and the permissions you choose:

  • Support data: name, email address and the contents of messages you choose to send through our support form.
  • Ride and location data: route, precise or approximate location, distance, duration, timestamps and related ride information when you activate a location-based feature.
  • Content you provide: ride titles, notes, photographs, videos, voice notes, saved location points and other material you choose to add to a session.
  • Device and technical data: device type, operating system, app version, language, diagnostic information, crash reports and security logs.
  • Usage data: interactions with app features and basic analytics used to understand and improve the Services.

We do not collect precise location, photos or other permission-based data unless you enable the relevant feature or permission. You can change permissions in your device settings.

3. When data is shared

We do not sell personal data. We may share limited data with:

  • Service providers that host data, deliver infrastructure, process diagnostics or help operate the Services under contractual confidentiality and security duties.
  • FormSubmit processes the details you enter in the protected support form so the message can be delivered to RideStory. Do not submit information that is not needed for your request.
  • Apple for App Store distribution and operating-system features, subject to Apple’s own privacy terms.
  • Authorities or professional advisers when reasonably necessary to comply with law, protect rights and safety, investigate misuse or establish legal claims.
  • A successor organisation in connection with a merger, financing, acquisition or sale of assets, subject to appropriate safeguards.

When you export or share a replay, video, GPX file or RideStory session, the selected content is disclosed to the destination and audience you choose. Review the destination before sharing.

4. International transfers

Our service providers may process data outside your country. Where data is transferred from the EEA, United Kingdom or Switzerland to a country without an adequacy decision, we use recognised safeguards such as the European Commission’s Standard Contractual Clauses and, for UK transfers, the UK International Data Transfer Addendum or another lawful transfer mechanism.

5. Data retention and deletion

RideStory does not create user accounts. Sessions and their content remain on your device until you delete them, remove the app or export them to a destination you choose. Support form messages may be retained for a limited period needed to answer the request, maintain security and meet legal obligations.

You can delete sessions and individual content using the controls available in the app. Removing the app deletes locally stored app data, subject to any device backups or copies you previously exported.

6. Security

We use reasonable technical and organisational measures designed to protect personal data, including access controls and secure transmission where appropriate. No system is completely secure, so we cannot guarantee absolute security.

7. Your privacy rights

Depending on where you live, you may have rights to access, correct, delete, restrict or object to processing of your personal data, receive a portable copy, withdraw consent and complain to a data protection authority. You may also have the right not to be subject to a decision based solely on automated processing that produces legal or similarly significant effects.

Submit requests through the support form and select “Privacy request”. We may ask for information reasonably needed to verify your identity. We will not discriminate against you for exercising a privacy right.

United Kingdom

8. Information for UK users

For users in the United Kingdom, we process personal data under the UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018. The legal bases in section 2 apply under UK law. UK users may exercise the rights listed in section 7 and may lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO) through ico.org.uk/make-a-complaint.

We would appreciate the opportunity to address your concern first. Use the support form and select “Privacy request”. International transfers of UK personal data use the safeguards described in section 4.

9. Children

The Services are not directed to children under 13, or a higher minimum age where required by local law. We do not knowingly collect personal data from a child below the applicable age without valid parental authorisation. A parent or guardian who believes a child has provided data can contact us to request deletion.

10. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy to reflect changes to the Services, law or our practices. We will update the date at the top and provide additional notice when required. Continued use after the effective date is subject to the updated policy.
